Akara

Brand guidelines for Akara by FlytBase. "Akara" means "to give shape" in Hindi/Sanskrit. These guidelines ensure consistent use of the brand across all touchpoints.

Logo

THE MARK

The Akara mark is a terrain cross-section forming the letter A. The ridge represents elevation data, the strata lines represent geological layers, the dashed crossbar is a survey reference line, and the summit marker is a geodetic control point.

Usage Rules

DO

Use the logo on cream, white, or dark backgrounds only. Maintain clear space around the mark.

DON'T

Place the logo on busy backgrounds, colored surfaces, or over photographs without a container.

DO

Use DM Serif Display for page titles and site/project names. Use Inter for everything else.

DON'T

Mix display and body fonts arbitrarily. Don't use DM Serif for small text or UI labels.

DO

Use golden amber (#C4A34A) sparingly for accents -- survey lines, altitude data, warnings.

DON'T

Use amber as a primary color. It's an accent that highlights, not dominates.

DO

Include the grain texture overlay and earthy gradient wash on backgrounds for texture.

DON'T

Make gradients too prominent. They should be felt, not seen -- subtle atmosphere, not decoration.
